# How Do 3D Glasses Work?

In the past few years 3D movies have made a tremendous comeback.

Last year alone, movie studios put out more than 40 3D films or re-releases compared with only one 3D movie as recently as 2008.

3D movies create an immersive experience for the audience.

Images appear to fill the empty space between the movie goers and the screen often with startling results.

All of them rely on your brain's binocular vision system, which takes 2D images from each eye and combines them in a way that lets you perceive depth and distance.

Let's take these colorful cardboard glasses that most people associate with 3D movies.

Each pair has one red lens and another that's cyan.

The red lens lets one eye see only red images.

The cyan lens picks up only bluish-green images.

translation

sentence:
在过去的几年里，3D电影已经卷土重来了。
仅去年一年，电影公司就推出或重新发行了40多部3D电影，而2008年只有一部3D电影。
3D电影为观众创造了身临其境的体验。
图像似乎填补了电影观众和屏幕之间的空白，往往会带来惊人的结果。
所有这些都依赖于你大脑的双目视觉系统，该系统从每只眼睛获取2D图像，并将它们组合在一起，让你感知深度和距离。
让我们以这些彩色的纸板眼镜为例，大多数人都会把它与3D电影联系在一起。
每一对都有一个红色的晶状体，另一个是青色的。
红色的晶状体让一只眼睛只能看到红色的图像。
青色透镜只能捕捉蓝绿色的图像。

On screen you're presented with 2 slightly offset images that were filmed separately.

One is red the other is cyan.

When your wearing your 3D glasses each eye sees only one of those images.

Your brain takes the information from the red image and from the cyan image and puts them together to create a three dimensional picture.

A more recent approach to making 3D movies is to use polarization rather than color separation.

A typical polarization technique is to have 2 synchronized projectors hit the screen at slightly different angles.

To get the 3D effect, you put on a pair of glasses that have polarizing lenses, which look way cooler than the colorful cardboard glasses.

Each lens allows images from only one of the projectors through to each eye.

Again, your brain fuses together the separate images to create the perception of depth on screen.

So the next time you enjoying a big budget hollywood blockbuster you'll know a little bit more about how that 3D magic was created.

sentence:
在屏幕上，你会看到两张分别拍摄的略微偏移的图像。
一个是红色，另一个是青色。
当你戴上3D眼镜时，每只眼睛只能看到其中的一个图像。
你的大脑从红色图像和青色图像中获取信息，并将它们组合在一起，创建一个三维图像。
最近制作3D电影的一种方法是使用偏振而不是分色。
典型的偏振技术是让2个同步投影仪以略微不同的角度撞击屏幕。
为了获得3D效果，你需要戴上一副带有偏光镜片的眼镜，这比彩色纸板眼镜酷多了。
每个镜头只允许图像从一个投影仪通过到每只眼睛。
同样，你的大脑将不同的图像融合在一起，在屏幕上创造出深度的感觉。
所以，下次你欣赏好莱坞大片的时候，你会对3D魔法是如何产生的有更多的了解。
