# mouse

The mouse is an electronic device used for input and navigation on a computer or other digital device. It consists of a small handheld unit with two buttons (left and right) and a pointer that moves across a flat surface, usually a monitor or screen.

translation

鼠标是用于在计算机或其他数字设备上输入和导航的电子设备。它由一个带有两个按钮(左和右)的小型手持装置和一个可以在平面上移动的指针组成，通常是显示器或屏幕。

The mouse was first introduced in the 1960s by Douglas Engelbart as part of his work at Stanford University. The original "mouse pointer" was designed to allow users to select text on a screen, but it quickly became a popular tool for performing other tasks as well. Today, mice are ubiquitous in both personal and professional computing environments.

translation

鼠标最初由道格拉斯·恩格尔巴特于20世纪60年代在斯坦福大学工作时引入。最初的“鼠标指针”被设计成允许用户在屏幕上选择文本，但它很快就成为了执行其他任务的流行工具。今天，鼠标在个人和专业计算环境中无处不在。

Mice come in many different designs and sizes, and some models offer additional features such as touchpads, scrolling wheels, and multiple buttons. They can connect to computers through USB ports or wirelessly using Bluetooth technology.

鼠标有许多不同的设计和大小，有些型号还提供了额外的功能，如触摸板、滚轮和多个按钮。他们可以通过 USB 接口或使用蓝牙技术无线连接到电脑。

In addition to being a convenient tool for navigating and manipulating information on a computer screen, mice have also become symbols of modern computing culture. Many people associate the use of a mouse with the experience of using a computer, and some even consider it an essential component of their daily lives.
