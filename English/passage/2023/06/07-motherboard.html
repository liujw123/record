# motherboard

A motherboard is a crucial component in any computer system. It is the main circuit board that connects all the different parts of the computer together, such as the central processing unit (CPU), memory (RAM), and storage devices. The motherboard acts as the "brain" of the computer, controlling and coordinating all the other components to ensure that they work correctly and efficiently.

One of the most important functions of the motherboard is to provide a platform for connecting all the peripherals, such as the keyboard, mouse, monitor, and speakers. It also provides a connection to the external hard drive or solid-state drive for additional storage space.

The motherboard also plays a critical role in managing power supply to all the components. It has a series of connectors and pins that connect to the power supply unit (PSU) to deliver electricity to the CPU, RAM, and other components.
