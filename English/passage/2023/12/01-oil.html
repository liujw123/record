# Will We Ever Run Out of Oil ?

Will we ever run out of oil?

The answer depends on your perspective.

If we are focusing on the stew of hydrocarbon molecules we collectively know as oil, the answer is probably no.

The earth crust holds literally trillions of barrels worth.

If we are focusing on our ability to make use of those hydrocarbons, the answer is probably yes.

We've already run out of the easiest oil to find and pump to the surface.

I'm talking about gushers like Spindletop in Texas, or the Ghawar oilfield in Saudi Arabia.

Today, the worlds uses roughly 75 million barrels of oil daily.

That's mostly to fuel our cars and trucks, but also for things like plastics and pesticides.

The world produces roughly 75 million barrels of oil per day, and there's roughly a trillion barrels of oil still waiting to be tapped in the ground.

我们会用完石油吗?
答案取决于你的角度。
如果我们关注的是我们统称为石油的碳氢化合物分子的混合物，答案可能是否定的。
地壳可以容纳数万亿桶的石油。
如果我们专注于我们利用这些碳氢化合物的能力，答案可能是肯定的。
我们已经耗尽了最容易找到的石油，并将其泵出地面。
我说的是德克萨斯州的Spindletop油田，或者沙特阿拉伯的Ghawar油田。
今天，全球每天消耗大约7500万桶石油。
这主要是为我们的汽车和卡车提供燃料，但也为塑料和农药等产品提供燃料。
全世界每天生产大约7500万桶石油，还有大约1万亿桶石油等待开采。

So there's plenty of oil for the immediate future.

Problem is, most of that oil is pretty hard to get out for a variety of reasons from geopolitical to geographical, so to be more costly to produce.

That said, there's no shortage of customers for oil.

And technologies like melting tar sand, flooding old fields with carbon dioxide, and even fracking, are allowing us to get more oil.

That's how the U.S. has reduced its oil imports in recent years.

Of course, even if we don't run out of oil in the near future, we might want to stop using it anyway.

Maybe because it's coming from countries that don't like us, or because of burning it produces climate change and air pollution, or there are the disasters, like BP's gulf oil spill.

In other words, we might want to end the age of oil before it gets any stickier.
