# What is the Higgs Boson ?

You may have heard about the Higgs Boson.

It's the particle that physicists have just discovered at the Large Hadron Collider -- the giant particle accelerator near Geneva.

Bu the Higgs isn't just any particle.

It's a whole new type of particle.

And it plays a very special role in the scheme of things.

So, here's the idea.

The Higgs particles make up a condensate, a type of liquid that fills space around us.

It's completely invisible and we move through it freely.

But we know it's there by the way the forces of nature operate.

The condensate is absolutely essential to our own existence.

The Higgs condensate makes the vacuum of space a physical medium.

And we know that any physical medium will affect the behaviour of forces.

translation

sentence:
你可能听说过希格斯玻色子。
这是物理学家刚刚在日内瓦附近的大型强子对撞机(Large Hadron Collider)发现的粒子。
但是希格斯粒子不是普通的粒子。
这是一种全新的粒子。
它在整个过程中扮演着非常特殊的角色。
这就是我的想法。
希格斯粒子构成了一种凝聚态，一种充满我们周围空间的液体。
它是完全看不见的，我们可以自由地穿过它。
但我们通过自然力量的运作方式知道它的存在。
凝聚态对我们的存在是绝对必要的。
希格斯凝聚态使空间真空成为物理介质。
我们知道任何物理介质都会影响力的行为。

In fact, the Higgs condensate is closely related to superconductors.

A superconductor blocks magnetism.

The Higgs condensate blocks a close relative of magnetism, known as the Weak Nuclear Force.

The Weak Force and Electromagnetism are symmetrical counterparts.

The Higgs cloaks their symmetry.

It bottles up the Weak Nuclear Force inside atomic nuclei and lets Electromagnetism roam free.

The Higgs affects particles that depend on the Weak Force.

For example, the Weak Nuclear Force can transmute an electron into a neutrino.

By bottleing up the Weak Force, the Higgs allows the electron and the neutrino to remain distinct under ordinary circumstances.

The Higgs also has another related effect.

It gives elementary particles their mass.

In other words, their inertia, their responsiveness to acceleration.

sentence:
事实上，希格斯凝聚态与超导体密切相关。
超导体阻挡磁性。
希格斯凝聚态阻挡了磁性的近亲，即弱核力。
弱力和电磁力是对称的对应物。
希格斯粒子掩盖了它们的对称性。
它将弱核力封闭在原子核内，让电磁自由活动。
希格斯粒子影响依赖弱力的粒子。
例如，弱核力可以将电子转化为中微子。
通过将弱力装瓶，希格斯粒子允许电子和中微子在一般情况下保持不同。
希格斯粒子还有另一个相关的效应。
它赋予基本粒子质量。
换句话说，它们的惯性，它们对加速度的响应。

Way back in the early Universe, just after the Big Bang, particles had no mass.

They all moved at the speed of light.

Then the Higgs condensed out.

It gave particles their mass and slowed them down.

That's what makes atoms possible.

It's what makes atomic nuclei stable.

That's just the start.

The Higgs and other particles of its type are responsible for all sorts of cosmic events.

Like the inbalance of matter and antimatter, and the acceleration of cosmic expansion, both in the distant past and today.

Above all, the Higgs, more than any other type of particle, is very sensitive to what happens at a deeper level of reality.

A level of reality that underlies the current particles and forces that we know about.
