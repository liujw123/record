# Why is HIV So Hard to Kill ?

HIV, the virus that causes AIDS, is a master of disguise.

It has the ability to change its outer coat of proteins frequently.

This quick-change act makes it hard for the immune system to recognise it and destroy it.

But what makes HIV even harder to kill is that it targets one of the most important cells in the immune system for fighting infection.

Technically called a CD4+ cell, this cell is more commonly known as a Helper T cell.

You can think of Helper T cells as the air traffic controllers of the immune system.

The immune system's job is to protect the body from dangerous traitors, like cancer, and harmful intruders, like viruses and bacteria.

Overlapping networks of immune cells are constantly talking to each other and coordinating their efforts.

It's a lot like airplanes zipping around in a busy airspace.

The Helper T cells sit at the heart of many of these networks.

translation

sentence:
导致艾滋病的HIV病毒是伪装大师。
它有能力频繁地改变它的外层蛋白质。
这种快速变化的行为使得免疫系统很难识别并摧毁它。
但让HIV更难被杀死的是，它的目标是免疫系统中对抗感染的最重要的细胞之一。
技术上称为CD4+细胞，这种细胞通常被称为辅助性T细胞。
你可以把辅助性T细胞想象成免疫系统的空中交通管制员。
免疫系统的工作是保护身体免受危险的叛徒，如癌症，和有害的入侵者，如病毒和细菌。
重叠的免疫细胞网络不断地相互交谈并协调它们的努力。
这很像飞机在繁忙的空域中快速飞行。
辅助性T细胞位于这些网络的核心。

fighting infection -> 抗感染：指身体对抗病原体入侵的过程，包括免疫系统的反应和药物治疗等手段。
Helper T cell -> 辅助性T细胞

It does things like tell one group of immune cells, the Killer T cells, to target HIV and destroy it.

Or Helper T cells tell another group of immune defenders, the so called B Cells, to make antibodies that attack the virus.

Now imagine if all the air traffic controllers started to disappear one by one.

None of the other cells in the remaining networks would know where to go or what to do.

The whole system would have trouble fighting not just HIV but many other illnesses as well.

Indeed, that's what we see in people with AIDS.

They become sick with ailments our immune system is usually better at fighting off.

Tuberculosis, meningitis, rare forms of pneumonia, non-Hodgkin's lymphoma, and cervical cancer.

HIV doesn't fight fair.

It attacks the very cells that would coordinate its destruction.

In order to boost the immune system against HIV, we need to get the Helper T cells back in control.
