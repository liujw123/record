# Animal Farm

# Chapter 9

Three days later it was announced that he had died in the hospital at Willingdon, in spite of receiving every attention a horse could have.

Squealer came to announce the news to the others. He had, he said, been present during Boxer's last hours.

“It was the most affecting sight I have ever seen!” said Squealer, lifting his trotter and wiping away a tear.

“I was at his bedside at the very last. And at the end, almost too weak to speak, he whispered in my ear that his sole sorrow was to have passed on before the windmill was finished. ‘Forward, comrades!' he whispered. ‘Forward in the name of the Rebellion. Long live Animal Farm! Long live Comrade Napoleon! Napoleon is always right.' Those were his very last words, comrades.”

Here Squealer's demeanour suddenly changed.

He fell silent for a moment, and his little eyes darted suspicious glances from side to side before he proceeded.

translation
  1. 三天后，有人宣布他已经在威灵顿的医院去世了，尽管他得到了马所能得到的一切照料。
  2. Squealer 来向其他动物宣布这个消息。他说，他一直在场，陪伴 Boxer 度过了最后的时光。
  3. “那是我所见过的最感人的场面！”Squealer 说着，抬起蹄子擦去一滴眼泪。
  4. “我一直守在他床边，直到最后一刻。临终时，他几乎虚弱得说不出话来，他在我耳边低声说，他唯一的遗憾就是在风车尚未建成前就离世了。‘前进，同志们！’他低语道，‘为了起义的名义前进。动物农庄万岁！拿破仑同志万岁！拿破仑永远是对的。’同志们，这就是他最后的话。”
  5. 就在这时，Squealer 的神情突然变了。
  6. 他沉默了一会儿，用他那双小眼睛疑虑地左右扫视了一圈，然后才继续说下去。
